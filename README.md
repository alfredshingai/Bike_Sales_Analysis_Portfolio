## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyses a dataset of 1,000 customers to uncover what drives bike purchases. The goal was to identify the highest-converting customer segments and generate actionable business recommendations — all built in Microsoft Excel.

The final workbook is structured as a professional, portfolio-ready deliverable with five dedicated sheets covering raw data, KPI summaries, pivot table analysis, visual charts, and written insights.

## 📈 Key Findings

### 1. 🧑‍💼 Middle Age is the Core Market
Customers aged 31–54 account for the overwhelming majority of bike buyers. Marketing campaigns should centre on this demographic.

### 2. 🌏 Pacific Region Has the Highest Conversion Rate
The Pacific region converts at ~59%, well above the global average of 48.1%. North America has the highest volume but lower conversion — a strong optimisation opportunity.

### 3. 💰 Income Drives Purchase Decisions
Bike buyers earn an average of ~$4,000 more annually than non-buyers. Premium offerings and flexible financing could unlock mid-income segments.

### 4. 💼 Professionals Purchase the Most
Professionals hold the highest buyer count and best conversion rate by occupation. Skilled Manual workers represent a large untapped group with targeted potential.

### 5. 🚗 Short Commuters Buy More Bikes
Customers with 0–1 mile commutes are the largest buyer group. Customers with long commutes (10+ miles) convert poorly — an e-bike or performance range strategy could address this.

### 6. 💍 Singles Convert Better Than Married Customers
Single customers convert at ~54% vs ~43% for married customers, likely due to fewer financial dependents.

### 7. 🎓 Higher Education = Higher Conversion
Bachelors and Graduate Degree holders purchase at significantly higher rates than those with Partial High School education, pointing to price sensitivity in lower-education groups.

---

## 🎯 Priority Customer Segment

> **Single | Middle-Aged | Professional | Income $60K–$80K | Pacific or North America**

Secondary target: Skilled Manual workers in Europe with short commutes — addressable through commuter-focused promotions.
